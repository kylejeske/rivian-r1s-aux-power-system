# Cable Fabrication Acceptance Guide

Use this guide for every cable made in-house. A cable is not ready for final assembly until every required check passes.

## Required Tools

- Cable cutter sized for 2 AWG through 14 AWG.
- Hydraulic or hex crimper with dies matching each lug/contact.
- Anderson contact crimp tool or verified compatible die set.
- Heat gun.
- Multimeter.
- Label maker or heat-shrink labels.
- Calipers or strip-length gauge.
- Adhesive-lined heat shrink.

## Required Records

Record each cable in the wire schedule before installation:

- Cable ID.
- Circuit name.
- Gauge.
- Color.
- Cable type.
- Length.
- Fuse/breaker protecting it.
- Source termination.
- Load termination.
- Lug/contact part numbers.
- Crimp die used.
- Inspection result.

## Fabrication Steps

1. Cut cable to measured length with service slack included.
2. Slide heat shrink and labels onto the cable before crimping the second end.
3. Strip insulation to the lug/contact manufacturer's strip length.
4. Inspect strands. Reject if strands are nicked, cut, corroded, or loose.
5. Insert conductor fully into lug/contact barrel.
6. Crimp using the die matched to the lug/contact and cable gauge.
7. Inspect crimp compression and conductor insertion depth.
8. Apply adhesive-lined heat shrink so the barrel and insulation transition are sealed.
9. Add circuit labels at both ends.
10. Perform acceptance checks.

## Acceptance Checks

Each cable must pass:

- Correct gauge, color, and length.
- Correct lug/contact on each end.
- No exposed conductor outside the lug/contact barrel except where the terminal design requires it.
- No cut or missing strands.
- Crimp is centered on the barrel and fully compressed.
- Heat shrink is sealed and adhesive has flowed.
- Labels are present and readable at both ends.
- Continuity check passes.
- Polarity check passes for assembled paired connectors.
- Pull check passes.

## Pull Check

Use a firm manual pull for all cables. Do not install any cable where the conductor moves inside the lug/contact.

For high-current cables, clamp or hold the terminal body and pull on the insulated cable with enough force to reveal a bad crimp without damaging a good one. If there is any movement, scrap the termination and remake the cable.

## Connector-Specific Checks

For Anderson SB contacts:

- Contact is the correct size for the wire gauge.
- Contact fully seats in the housing.
- Contact cannot be pulled out by hand after insertion.
- Positive and negative are installed in the intended orientation.
- Housing color/key matches the circuit.

For ring lugs:

- Stud hole matches the busbar, fuse, breaker, shunt, or switch stud.
- Lug sits flat without bending the cable.
- Heat shrink does not interfere with lug seating.
- Boot or cover fits after installation.

## Rejection Criteria

Reject and remake the cable if:

- Wrong wire gauge.
- Wrong lug/contact.
- Wrong polarity.
- Loose crimp.
- Nicked or missing strands.
- Heat-damaged insulation.
- Cracked lug barrel.
- Contact does not fully seat.
- Label is missing or ambiguous.
- Cable is too short to avoid strain.

## Commissioning Follow-Up

After first sustained load test:

- Inspect every high-current termination.
- Check for heat discoloration, softened insulation, loose hardware, or shifted strain relief.
- Measure or image temperature at SB120 contacts, fuse holders, breakers, shunt, busbars, and lugs.
- Remake any cable or termination that runs noticeably hotter than comparable conductors.
